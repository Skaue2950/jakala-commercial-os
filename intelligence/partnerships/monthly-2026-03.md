## Partners to Avoid This Quarter

### Accenture / Capgemini (SI Consulting)
These are the most obvious "complementary" SI partners on paper, but they are JAKALA's **direct competitors** for the same enterprise transformation budgets at H&M, Elkjøp, Pandora, and Bestseller. Any co-delivery arrangement would position JAKALA as a sub-contractor, eroding margin and brand positioning. More critically, Accenture's Song practice and Capgemini's Invent unit now actively pitch composable commerce and AI transformation — the exact GTM surface JAKALA is building. **Verdict:** Monitor as competitive intelligence, not partnership candidates. Conflict Risk: **2/10**.

### Salesforce Commerce Cloud (SFCC)
SFCC is technically in the partner universe, but its total cost of ownership is increasingly a barrier for Nordic mid-market accounts — the primary JAKALA ICP. More importantly, Salesforce's own Professional Services arm and preferred SIs (Accenture, Deloitte Digital) crowd out independent partners on large deals. JAKALA's Shopify and commercetools positioning would be actively undermined by co-marketing with SFCC, sending a confused platform message to prospects like Imerco and Sport Outlet who are currently evaluating alternatives. **Verdict:** Avoid active investment. Pursue only if an inbound SFCC opportunity arises from an existing key account. Conflict Risk: **3/10**.

### Sitecore
Sitecore's ongoing financial restructuring and customer churn across the Nordics creates reputational risk for any JAKALA partnership. Several Nordic accounts are actively migrating *away* from Sitecore toward Contentful, Storyblok, or Optimizely — a trend JAKALA should be capturing, not swimming against. Positioning alongside Sitecore would signal to forward-looking CDOs and CTOs that JAKALA is anchored to legacy DXP thinking. **Verdict:** Do not invest in Sitecore partnership. Position JAKALA as the migration partner *from* Sitecore instead. Conflict Risk: **4/10** (low conflict, but high brand positioning risk).
