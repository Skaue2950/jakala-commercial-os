## GTM Strategy: Data Revenue Unlock → AI Readiness Accelerator

---

## Entry Offer

**Data Revenue Diagnostic — €60–100K**

Assess: product data completeness across 60,000+ SKUs from 1,000+ brands, taxonomy consistency across 8 markets and languages, search relevance on lyko.com, and personalization activation capability for existing customer base.

**Framing:** "You've built the Nordics' leading beauty ecommerce platform and expanded to 8 markets. At 60,000 SKUs across 1,000 brands in 8 languages, product data quality and taxonomy consistency become the silent conversion killers."

**Price range:** €60–100K diagnostic → €300–600K Speedtrain + personalization program

---

## Why Now

- Multi-market expansion to 8 countries creates catalog complexity that scales exponentially — each new market adds translation, regulatory attribute, and taxonomy risk
- 60,000+ SKUs from 1,000+ brands = heavy reliance on supplier-provided data — quality is inconsistent across brands
- Own brands growing (10+) — own-brand product data requires internal governance that likely doesn't exist at scale
- Warehouse automation completed July 2025 — operational investment is made, next growth lever is digital product discovery
- Revenue at 750M SEK growing above industry average — but beauty ecommerce is hyper-competitive (Sephora, Douglas, Zalando Beauty entering Nordic)
- Avensia SCOPE platform is mature — the question is whether the data layer above it is performing

---

## Positioning

Position JAKALA as the **specialist data and personalization layer above their Avensia platform** — not competing with Avensia, not replacing the platform.

Key messages:
1. "Your Avensia platform is the right foundation. The 60,000-SKU catalog from 1,000 brands is where the gap is — brand data quality is rarely consistent across suppliers."
2. "8 markets means 8 languages and potentially 8 different attribute structures. A diagnostic shows you exactly where catalog inconsistency is costing you in search relevance and conversion."
3. "Your own brands are a margin opportunity — but own-brand product data governance is different from third-party data feeds. We can close that gap."

Do NOT:
- Position against Avensia (existing partner — do not threaten the relationship)
- Lead with platform replacement or DXP
- Pitch generic AI — Peter Gunnarsson (CTO) is technical and will see through it

## Competitive Considerations

- **Avensia:** Active platform partner. JAKALA plays above the platform — data intelligence and personalization layer.
- **Suppliers (1,000+ brands):** Data quality is supplier-dependent. JAKALA's value is normalizing across all of them.
- **Competitors (Sephora, Douglas, Zalando Beauty):** Entering Nordic market — urgency for Lyko to differentiate on product discovery and personalization.
