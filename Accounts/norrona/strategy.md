## Entry Offer

## Why Now

- US market expansion is underway (Boulder, CO office open) — multi-market platform requirements are growing
- New Oslo flagship and HQ opened — brand investment is significant. Digital D2C should match brand quality.
- D2C revenue $19M online (2024) — growing but below brand potential. Platform may be limiting conversion.
- No-discount policy = conversion depends entirely on product presentation and discovery quality — not promotions
- Technical products (ski, outdoor) = complex attribute requirements. Poor search = lost conversions.
- Family-owned business = long-term investment horizon. They invest when the case is compelling, not chasing trends.
- Upcycled collection lines = new product types requiring taxonomy and data workflow updates

## Positioning

Position JAKALA as a **brand-quality ecommerce partner for premium outdoor D2C** — not a generic agency.

Key messages:
1. "Your Oslo flagship is world-class. Your online store should be the same. A Check-up tells you whether your current platform can deliver that — or what it would take to get there."
2. "No-discount brand = you can't rely on promotions to drive conversion. Product presentation, search quality, and the shopping experience are your only levers."
3. "US expansion means multi-market, multi-currency, multi-size-standard. That complexity is where legacy platforms start to crack."

Reference JAKALA credentials:
- Shopify Premier Agency (Italy)
- Clients: Kiton, La Prairie, Pomellato — premium brands, similar positioning to Norrona

Do NOT:
- Lead with data or AI — they are a product brand, not a data company
- Pitch DXP or platform suite
- Position as a "digital transformation" partner — they think in brand terms, not tech terms
