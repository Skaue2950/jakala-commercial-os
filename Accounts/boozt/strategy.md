## GTM Strategy: Data Revenue Unlock → AI Readiness Accelerator

---

## Entry Offer

**Data Revenue Diagnostic**

Assess how product data quality and catalog completeness is limiting search relevance, personalisation accuracy, and conversion — specifically across Boozt's growing non-fashion categories.

**Framing:** "You're automating product data quality with Google AI — we can help you close the gaps your automation isn't catching yet, and quantify what they're costing you in conversion."

**Price range:** €60–120K diagnostic → €400–700K programme

---

## Why Now

- Boozt Tech published an article in November 2025 on automating product image and data quality with Google AI — the problem is acknowledged and actively worked on internally
- Revenue growth guidance narrowed to 0–3% for 2025 — conversion optimisation pressure is real
- Non-fashion categories now 42% of revenue — complex product attributes (home, sport, beauty) are harder to manage than fashion
- Club Boozt at 2M+ members — personalisation is rich in customer data but constrained by product data completeness
- Premium brand expansion (Birkenstock, Hunter Boots added 2025/26) — new brand onboarding creates data quality risk at catalog entry
- Per Boozt Tech blog (Feb 2026): AI-powered services journey is ongoing — external collaboration is part of their model (Google Cloud)

---

## Positioning

Position JAKALA as a **specialist layer on top of their Google Cloud investment** — not a competitor to their internal team or Google partnership.

Key messages:
1. "Your Google AI data quality automation is the right direction — we specialise in the gaps automation misses: attribute completeness, taxonomy consistency, cross-category discovery logic"
2. "With 42% non-fashion revenue and growing, your catalog complexity has moved beyond fashion expertise. Product data for home, sport, and beauty works differently."
3. "A focused diagnostic quantifies the revenue you're leaving on the table — and maps where your existing AI investment is being constrained by upstream data."

Do NOT:
- Challenge their internal tech culture or proprietary platform pride
- Position as an alternative to Google Cloud
- Lead with platform replacement or DXP — too early, wrong angle
- Use generic "AI transformation" language — they are a tech company and will see through it
