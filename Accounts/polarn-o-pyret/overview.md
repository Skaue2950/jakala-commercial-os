## Digital & Tech Snapshot

Polarn O. Pyret runs on Optimizely (Episerver) Digital Experience Platform, implemented by Avensia. The implementation is mobile-first and includes a unified commerce solution giving store staff 360-degree customer views. They have launched a successful second-hand platform ("PO.P Second Hand") for circular commerce — both online in Sweden and UK. They won Drapers Award for Best Circularity Initiative and Most Innovative UK Retailer.

## Key Signals

- **Optimizely platform is live and mature** — the platform is in place, but a DXP this size has a significant data layer above it. Personalization activation is likely limited.
- **Second-hand platform is a new product type** — different catalog attributes, condition grading, sustainability metadata. Product data governance challenge.
- **Multi-market (8+ countries)** — multi-language, multi-currency, per-market catalog variants. Classic data complexity.
- **Children's seasonal catalog** — high product turnover, seasonal drops, age/size attributes. Taxonomy quality matters for search and discovery.
- **RNB parent under financial pressure** — investment decisions require clear ROI framing.
- **Avensia is the existing partner** — JAKALA must position above the platform layer (data, personalization) not as a competing implementation agency.
- **Second-hand growth** — circular commerce creates new personalization and recommendation opportunities (used + new product pairing).
